USS Hornet Museum

Located on the southernmost pier of the former Naval Air Station Alameda in Alameda, California.

Photo Op

USS Hornet (CV/CVA/CVS-12) is an Essex-class aircraft carrier built for the United States Navy (USN) during World War II. She was decommissioned in 1970.

T-28c Trojan Trainer

The T-28c is the Navy's version of North American's Trojan trainer, with a tailhook and shorter propeller blades for landing on a carrier.

Salt Takes Its Toll

Because this is a museum, not a working warship, there is no horde of sailors to keep everything salt free, and there is a lot of rust.

A Sense of Proportion

This shot gives a good feel for just how large the flightdeck of the Hornet really is. It is freakin' massive!

Gemini Boilerplate MSC-307

A dummy capsule used by recovery forces for splashdown training in preparation for the actual Gemini missions.

Grumman TBF Avenger

The Avenger was a torpedo bomber that entered U.S. service in 1942, and first saw action during the Battle of Midway.

Apollo Test Capsule CM-011

USS Hornet served as the recovery ship for CM-011 for its AS-202 mission, off of Wake Island on August 25, 1966.

Vought F-8 Crusader

It takes a lot of engine to get to mach 1.8. Well, this is a lot of engine right there. This place is a playground.

Lockheed S-3 Viking

The Viking is a four-crew, twin-engine turbofan-powered jet aircraft used by the U.S.N. for antisubmarine missions.

A Trip To The "Head"

The bathrooms ("heads") onboard were not designed with comfort in mind. This is after they were upgraded in the 60's.

Grumman F-14 Tomcat

Everyone's favorite from the original "Top Gun" movie, the F-14 first flew 21 December 1970 and deployed on USS Enterprise in 1974.

Douglas A-4 Skyhawk

The black and white thing is the arresting hook: the only thing that slows the plane from 250 knots to a stop in about 200 feet.
